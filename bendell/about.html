Her Story

A Fighter Born from Experience

Heather Bendl isn't running for office because politics is a career path. She is running because she has seen how broken government can hurt real families — and she refuses to be silent about it.

A longtime resident of Edgewater, Florida, Heather has built her life in the heart of Volusia and Brevard counties. She is a mother of four, a high school marine biology educator in Florida's public schools, a civil rights advocate, a podcast host, and a courageous whistleblower who has spent years exposing corruption in Florida's family court system.

Educator First

Heather's daily classroom work as a high school marine biology teacher gives her something most politicians simply don't have: a front-row seat to the real challenges facing Florida students, parents, and teachers. She understands that real educational reform comes from listening to those in the trenches — not from bureaucrats in Tallahassee who have never set foot in a public school classroom.

She has applied for science grants from organizations like Lockheed Martin and Duke Energy to give her students hands-on learning opportunities — because she believes that when the government won't fund something vital, you find another way.

Civil Rights Advocate & Whistleblower

Heather's advocacy goes far beyond the classroom. She is a family court and CPS (Child Protective Services) corruption whistleblower who has faced the system head-on, challenging court orders that she argues were issued without proper legal authority and in violation of constitutional Due Process rights.

She has active appeals in both Florida and Illinois, fighting for the principle that geography and Due Process matter — that no court can simply disregard the law when it becomes inconvenient. Her courage in exposing this systemic corruption has made her a voice for thousands of Florida families who have felt abandoned by a system that should protect them.

Community Leader

When Hurricane Milton struck in 2024 and Heather saw that Edgewater was missing a Community Emergency Response Team, she didn't wait for someone else to fix it. She worked directly with community members to establish a CERT program for Edgewater — because real leadership means stepping up when your community needs you, not waiting for a government program to save the day.

Why District 30?

Florida House District 30 encompasses communities across Volusia and Brevard County — communities Heather has lived in, taught in, and raised her family in. She knows the local challenges: the insurance crisis crushing homeowners, the need to protect the Indian River Lagoon and Mosquito Lagoon, the importance of keeping Florida's schools strong, and the critical need for government accountability.

Heather is running against incumbent Chase Tramont in the Republican primary on August 18, 2026. She is not a career politician and doesn't plan to become one. She is running to serve, solve real problems, and come home.
