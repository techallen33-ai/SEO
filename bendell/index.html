Why Heather Bendl?

A Voice for Everyday Floridians

Heather is not a career politician. She is a teacher, a mother, and a fighter who has spent years standing up for families and communities across Volusia and Brevard County.

Educator at Heart

As a high school marine biology teacher in Florida public schools, Heather knows firsthand what our students and teachers need to thrive — and what broken policies look like from the inside.

Civil Rights Advocate

Heather has battled family court corruption and government overreach firsthand. She understands due process, constitutional rights, and the importance of holding institutions accountable.

Community Leader

After Hurricane Milton, Heather took action to establish Edgewater's first Community Emergency Response Team. She doesn't wait for permission — she gets things done.

Campaign Platform

Issues That Matter to Our District

Heather is focused on the real challenges facing families in Volusia and Brevard County — and she has the life experience to solve them.

Education & Teacher Support

Competitive teacher pay, school safety, protecting parental rights, and giving schools the resources they need to succeed.

Family Court & CPS Reform

Transparency, due process, and accountability in Florida's family courts and child protective services to protect children and parents.

Environmental Protection

Protecting the Indian River Lagoon, Mosquito Lagoon, and coastal ecosystems that make Volusia and Brevard County special.

Property Insurance Relief

Fighting the insurance crisis destroying Florida homeowners with real solutions — not corporate giveaways or empty promises.

Government Accountability

As a whistleblower, Heather knows how to identify corruption and demand transparency from government agencies and public officials.

Community Emergency Preparedness

Building local resilience — supporting CERT programs, first responders, and emergency infrastructure after hard lessons from recent hurricanes.

About the Candidate

Meet Heather Bendl

Heather Bendl is an Edgewater, Florida resident — a high school marine biology educator, a dedicated mother of four, a civil rights advocate, and a whistleblower who has spent years exposing corruption in Florida's family court system.

She holds a Bachelor of Science degree and has dedicated her career to shaping young minds in Florida's public school system. When she saw injustice, she didn't stay silent — she fought back, taking on the court system and championing the rights of families across Florida and Illinois.
