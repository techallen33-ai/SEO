Retirement Planning Strategies in Edgewater, FL

Thinking about retirement in Volusia County? Here's what to consider.

Edgewater, Florida is a great place to retire — or to plan for retirement. The combination of no state income tax, a lower cost of living compared to much of the country, and proximity to the coast makes Volusia County an increasingly popular destination for retirees relocating from states like New York, New Jersey, and the Midwest.

But a good location is only part of the picture. Retirement planning requires careful thought about income, taxes, healthcare, and longevity — and the decisions you make in the years leading up to retirement often have the biggest impact.

Key Retirement Planning Considerations

1 When to Claim Social Security

One of the most consequential decisions you'll make is when to begin taking Social Security benefits. You can claim as early as age 62, but your monthly benefit increases significantly the longer you wait — up to age 70. The right answer depends on your health, other income sources, and whether you're married. There is no one-size-fits-all answer.

2 Managing Your Retirement Income Tax Picture

Many retirees are surprised to discover that retirement income isn't necessarily tax-free. Depending on the source — 401(k) withdrawals, Social Security, pensions, or investment accounts — a meaningful portion of your income may still be taxable at the federal level. Florida's lack of a state income tax helps, but federal taxes still apply. Planning your withdrawal strategy in advance can make a real difference.

3 Roth Conversions Before Retirement

The years between retiring and claiming Social Security can create a window of lower taxable income — which some people use strategically to convert traditional IRA or 401(k) funds into a Roth IRA. This isn't right for everyone, but it's worth understanding as part of a broader tax-aware retirement strategy.

4 Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)

If you have a traditional IRA or 401(k), the IRS requires you to begin taking distributions at a certain age. Failing to plan for RMDs can result in unexpected taxable income and potential penalties. Understanding when your RMDs begin and how much they'll be is an important part of retirement income planning.

5 Healthcare Before Medicare

If you retire before age 65, you'll need to bridge the gap before Medicare coverage begins. This is an often-overlooked cost that can significantly affect your retirement budget. Knowing your options — including marketplace plans and COBRA — is an important part of early retirement planning.

6 Longevity and Sequence of Returns

Retirement can last 20, 25, or even 30 years. That means your financial plan needs to account for the possibility of a long retirement — including the risk that a market downturn early in retirement could have an outsized impact on your savings. This is sometimes called sequence of returns risk, and it's one reason why having a thoughtful withdrawal strategy matters.

Retirement Planning in Edgewater and the Surrounding Area

Whether you're already living in Edgewater, New Smyrna Beach, Port Orange, or elsewhere in Volusia County — or you're planning a move to the area from out of state — the retirement planning questions are largely the same. What changes is your specific situation: your savings, your income sources, your timeline, and your goals.
